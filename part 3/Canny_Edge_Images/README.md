# Canny Edge Detector — Performance Under Noisy Conditions

When run on the noisy image without the Gaussian smoothing stage, Canny produces noticeably more false edges than its smoothed counterpart, with short, fragmented edge segments scattered throughout flat regions where noise gradients exceed the lower hysteresis threshold. Edge quality remains sharper and thinner than Sobel's because non-maximum suppression still narrows responses to single-pixel ridges, but many of those ridges correspond to noise rather than real structure. Continuity of true object boundaries is partially maintained by hysteresis linking, yet boundaries are frequently broken where noise disturbs the local gradient direction, causing the suppression step to discard valid edge pixels. Noise sensitivity is therefore high without pre-filtering — Canny depends on the Gaussian step to stabilize gradient orientation and magnitude before thresholding. With smoothing reintroduced, the same image yields cleaner, more continuous, and far less speckled edges, illustrating that the Gaussian stage is integral to Canny's noise robustness rather than optional.
